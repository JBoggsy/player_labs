4 · Surface-level dig: where we're leaking games

The single loudest signal (crew): crewborg skips votes ~2× as often as top crew (1.76 vs 0.86 skips/game, d=+0.78), calls ~half the meetings (0.30 vs 0.71, d=−0.65), and casts ~half the player-votes (0.64 vs 1.35, d=−0.61) — despite chatting MORE (d=+0.38) and completing more tasks (6.79 vs 6.10). We talk and do tasks but won't pull the voting trigger. Corroboration: 256 of our clean crew games were losses where we'd completed 6+ of 8 tasks — tasks aren't the bottleneck; converting reads into ejections is.

Imposter: crewborg gets the same kill count as the top imposters (1.77 vs 1.78) but follows/tails 37% less (d=−0.80), chases 35% less, chats 39% less and stays silent in more meetings (d=−0.46), and gets its first kill ~20% later (d=+0.36). Same kills, quieter and more passive social+hunting game.

Caveat — data quality

Of 1,500 requested games, 772 (51%) were dead connect-timeouts — a crew seat's container failed to start (no gameplay, auto-scored an imposter win). Those are dropped here; all stats are on the 728 fully-played games. crewborg's own connect-timeout rate was 24% (heavy LLM cold-start vs a tight platform deadline) — you noted this is now fixed, so a fresh batch should retain the full N and tighten every CI on this page.
