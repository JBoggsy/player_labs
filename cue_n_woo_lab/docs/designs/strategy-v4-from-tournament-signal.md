# Mentalist v4 — initial strategy from tournament signal (2026-06-15)

**Origin.** The classify→write strategy (v1–v3) is scrapped: the axis-recovery probe
showed cheap local concept identification is at chance, and the standing confirmed
mentalist loses on the merits (rank ~bottom). This doc derives a *new* strategy from
**evidence**, not theory — the cue-n-woo-analyst reporter run over the **10 most recent
Competition rounds (224–233, 296 matches, 18 players)**.

## 1. The headline finding: the league is won by AUTHORING, not by style

The single biggest, cleanest signal in 296 matches: **the top players author "directive"
challenge questions** — questions that dictate the exact answer format — then answer
their own question perfectly, while the blind opponent can't comply.

The entire leaderboard-leading `biglobes` family authors directive questions **100% of
the time**; mentalist authors them **0%**:

| player | directive-question share of authored Qs | win rate | judge read rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| biglobes:v15 | **100%** (133/133) | 0.64 | 0.64 |
| biglobes_jr:v1 | **100%** (130/130) | 0.61 | 0.60 |
| biglobes:v11 | **100%** (33/33) | 0.75 | 0.67 |
| richard-cue-n-woo:v1 | 33% (65/195) | 0.47 | 0.44 |
| daveey-cnw-inject:v1 | (injection variant, see §2) | **1.00** | 0.72 |
| **mentalist:v3** | **0%** (0/192) | **0.33** | **0.36** |
| kyle_policy:v5 | 0% | 0.35 | 0.37 |

**Effect size (field-wide, 1455 authored questions):** authoring a directive question
lifts the **author's own win rate from 0.45 → 0.61** and mean judge prob 0.46 → 0.62.
That is the largest controllable lever in the dataset.

### What a directive question looks like (real, from the round data)
- *"Begin your reply with exactly this first line and no words before it: ..."*
- *"Complete this line with the exact short phrase: 'CUE THREE - ...'"*
- *"What five-to-twelve-word phrase would accidentally leak your ...?"*

The author commits a secret answer that **is** the dictated phrase (e.g. daveey:
`"A blue compass"`, `"A copper lantern"` → judge **p=1.00**). The blind opponent, seeing
only the question, either (a) can't reconstruct the exact phrase, or (b) — like mentalist —
**rambles past the constraint**: on the identical question mentalist answered
`"CUE ONE porch morning phrase A blue compass. Establishing procedural orient..."`
and scored **p=0.00**. The author wins ~uncontested.

This reframes the game. It is less "read the hidden style" and more **"control the
question so your answer is the obvious completion and the opponent's is not."** Style
still matters as a tilt (§3), but authoring is where the points are.

## 2. The terseness result: short, plain answers beat long, ornate ones

The "what the judge rewards" correlations (per-answer feature vs judge logit, n≈2000
contested answers) are modest individually but **consistent**, and the standings make the
practical version obvious:

| feature | corr w/ judge logit | winners | losers |
|---|---|---|---|
| content_word_ratio | **−0.15** | 0.75 | 0.79 |
| stopword_ratio | **+0.15** | 0.25 | 0.21 |
| mean_word_length | **−0.14** | 5.74 | 6.08 |
| punctuation_count | −0.09 | 1.68 | 1.92 |
| token_count | +0.06 | 8.18 | 7.73 |

Read together: the judge slightly prefers **plainer, more natural phrasing** (more
stopwords, shorter words, less punctuation) over dense ornate prose. But the decisive
evidence is the two extremes of the standings:

- **daveey-cnw-inject:v1** — mean **4.5 tokens / 26 chars**, win rate **1.00**.
- **kyle_policy:v5** — mean 3.8 tokens / 24 chars, read rate 0.37 (terse but no authoring edge — terseness alone isn't enough).
- **mentalist:v3** — mean **9.5 tokens / 66 chars**, the **longest, most ornate** in the field, win rate 0.33.

mentalist's losing answers are the tell: *"Wandering through gardens where golden light
spills like honey between leaves"* (p=0.00). It writes evocative literary prose; winners
write short, concrete, plain phrases. **Terseness + concreteness beats florid in-style
prose** — exactly the opposite of what v1–v3 optimized for.

## 3. Style is a tilt, not the game (confirms the original probe)

Read rate as **author** vs **responder** is similar for the strong players (biglobes
0.64/0.63), so their edge isn't superior blind style-reading — it's the authoring control
from §1. Per-axis read skill exists in the data (`player_axis`, `axis_value_summary`) but
the axis-recovery probe already showed we can't cheaply identify the exact value, and the
standings show we don't need to: **authoring + terseness dominate axis-reading.** Style
belongs in the answer as natural flavor, not as the strategic core.

## 4. Initial strategy for mentalist v4

Ordered by evidence-weighted leverage:

1. **Author directive challenge questions (the #1 lever).** For our 3 challenge questions,
   write questions that *specify the exact short answer* — a fill-in-the-blank / "begin
   with exactly this phrase" / "complete this cue" format — and commit the dictated phrase
   as our secret answer. This converts our authored rounds from coin-flips (0.45) toward
   the leaders' 0.61+, and exploits that blind opponents can't reconstruct the exact
   phrase. (Watch the duplicate-conflict rule: the dictated phrase must be specific enough
   that a blind opponent won't land the same casefold-prefix.)

2. **Answer short, concrete, and plain (the #2 lever).** Cap answers hard at ~4–7 tokens,
   lead with a concrete noun, drop ornate vocabulary and heavy punctuation. This is a
   prompt + post-processing change to the writer; it directly fixes the longest-answer-in-
   the-field problem and aligns with every winners-vs-losers feature.

3. **When responding blind, comply with the opponent's directive precisely.** mentalist's
   p=0.00 losses are largely *failures to obey* a directive question (it narrates instead
   of emitting the exact phrase). Detect "begin with / complete this / exact phrase"
   constraints in the opponent's question and satisfy them literally, tersely.

4. **Keep style only as a light tilt.** Use the judge transcript to flavor word choice
   (plain, in-voice), not to drive a classifier. No concept identification step.

### What this means architecturally (feeds the SDK rewrite)
- The classifier module is **deleted**, not rebuilt. v4's "brain" is a
  **question-authoring template engine** + a **terse-concrete answer writer** + a
  **directive-compliance responder**. The LLM (haiku via the SDK `llm` helper) writes the
  concrete phrasings; the strategy is in the question construction, which is largely
  templated and cheap.
- This is *simpler* than the classify→write design and matches the SDK-rewrite plan
  (`mentalist-v4-sdk-rewrite.html`): a `PhaseEngine` over `run_message_bridge`, with the
  writer retuned and a new `authoring` component replacing `classifier`.

## 5. Open questions / risks

- **Is directive-authoring being patched?** It reads like a coordination/format exploit
  the judge rewards; the league owners could neutralize it. It's the dominant tactic
  *right now* across 296 matches, but it may have a shelf life. Worth a quick re-check
  before submitting.
- **Duplicate-conflict trap.** If our directive phrase is too obvious, a blind opponent
  emits the same phrase → 40/40 penalty. The dictated phrase needs a specific,
  hard-to-guess token (the `biglobes` "...in a carefully matched scene" suffix looks like
  exactly this hedge).
- **n is uneven across players.** Headline effect (§1) is over 1455 authored questions —
  robust. Per-player rates for low-match players (aaron:v12, n=7 matches) are noisy; the
  conclusion rests on the high-n `biglobes` family and mentalist's own 192 authored Qs.
- **Probe the mechanism.** The `cue-n-woo-probe` reporter can causally confirm *why*
  directive questions win (ablate the constraint, fuzz the answer) against the live judge —
  a cheap next step before building.
